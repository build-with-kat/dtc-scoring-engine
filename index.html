The Problem

Not all inbound leads are equal

AirOps receives inbound demo requests from DTC brands. Not every lead has the same potential to convert, and sales rep time is finite. Without a systematic way to prioritize, reps rely on gut feel or submission order. High-intent leads go cold while time is spent on brands that are not ready.

This engine scores every inbound lead on fit and timing the moment it arrives, assigns a priority level, and routes the right action automatically. The result: reps focus on the leads most likely to convert, sequences handle everyone else, and nothing falls through the cracks.

Architecture Overview

Five stages, one loop

Every inbound lead passes through enrichment and scoring automatically. The final stage is what separates this from a static model: outcomes and rep notes feed back in, signal weights update, and all leads re-score.

Scoring Model

Fit × Timing

Each lead receives two independent scores out of 100. Fit 75 or above AND Timing 75 or above = P1. High fit with low timing means the right brand but not ready yet. High timing with low fit means ready but not the right brand. The point values below are starting assumptions that will be refined as conversion data accumulates.

P1 to P4

What happens to each lead

Only P1 requires proactive rep outreach. P2 runs on an automated sequence. P3 and P4 receive no demo booking invitation. If a P2 lead books through the automated Calendly link, the rep handles the call.

On P1 outreach

For P1 leads a personalized email converts better than a fully automated one. The engine uses Claude to draft an email based on the signals that fired: naming the specific competitor appearing in ChatGPT, referencing the AEO role just posted, noting the pricing page visits. Rep receives the morning Slack digest with P1 leads listed and drafted emails ready. Rep reviews, edits if needed, and sends. Rep blocks 30 minutes each morning for this before anything else.

Build Plan

Validate in Clay,
then build the engine

The custom engine is the goal. Clay is how we validate the signal model without committing engineering resources upfront. Two things Clay cannot do that matter:

  • Tables become unreliable past 20k rows and Clay has no durable store for signal history. You cannot look back at what signals a lead had at inbound time when a deal closes months later.
  • Re-scoring in Clay requires a manual batch run. A new timing signal on an existing lead will not automatically update their score until the next run.
Phase 1 · Weeks 1 to 6
Clay
Clay pulls enrichment data (BuiltWith, Similarweb, LinkedIn via Clay's data providers) and runs the scoring formula as a set of columns. P1 to P4 assigned. Salesforce updated. Morning Slack digest fires automatically. Rep notes and converted Y/N tracked manually.
No additional infrastructure. Live within days using the existing Clay account.
Phase 2 · Months 2 to 6, once Clay is validated
Custom Engine
Supabase stores accounts, signals, scores, and full signal history. n8n orchestrates enrichment triggers, scoring runs, Salesforce updates, and Slack alerts. BuiltWith and Similarweb called directly via their APIs. LinkedIn data via a licensed provider. Claude Cowork drafts personalized P1 outreach emails from scorecard signals and runs the AI search gap check automatically for each new inbound lead.
Direct API calls replace Clay enrichment. Signal history accumulates from this point forward.
Signal Learning

How the model gets smarter

The model starts with assumptions. Over time those assumptions are replaced by data.

Deal outcomes
When a deal closes or is lost, the signals that lead had at inbound time are reviewed against the result. Signals associated with wins get more weight. Signals that did not predict conversion get less. First meaningful review at around 30 closes.
Rep notes
Reps add notes explaining why a lead did or did not progress. Trends in those notes flag false positives. If the same objection keeps appearing across P1 leads, a signal is misleading and needs correcting.
Signal updates
New signals are added as trends emerge and underperforming signals are removed. All active leads re-score automatically after each model update.
